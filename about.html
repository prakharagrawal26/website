About Me

I am a Prime Minister's Research Fellow (PMRF) pursuing a PhD in Geophysics at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bengaluru. My research passion lies at the intersection of large-scale computational modeling, high-performance computing (HPC), and data science.

My doctoral work involves developing and utilizing complex physics-based simulation codes (primarily in Fortran and C++, parallelized with MPI and OpenMP) to model the interiors of planets and understand phenomena like magnetic field generation. This requires managing and running simulations on powerful supercomputer clusters and subsequently analyzing terabytes of resulting data.

This hands-on experience has honed my skills not only in scientific computing and numerical methods but also extensively in data analysis and programming. I am proficient in Python (including libraries like NumPy, Pandas, SciPy, Matplotlib, Scikit-learn, TensorFlow/Keras), C++, Fortran, SQL, and adept at working within Linux/HPC environments. I leverage these skills to extract meaningful insights from complex, high-dimensional datasets, often employing statistical analysis and machine learning techniques for tasks like time series analysis and pattern recognition within simulation outputs.

Prior to my PhD, I completed my B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from MANIT Bhopal and gained valuable industry experience as a Production Planning Engineer at Honda Cars, where I applied data-driven forecasting to optimize processes.

Driven by a desire to apply my analytical and computational skills to solve diverse real-world problems, I am actively seeking opportunities in Data Science, Machine Learning, and related Software Engineering roles. I am enthusiastic about tackling challenges involving big data, building predictive models, and contributing to innovative AI-powered solutions.
